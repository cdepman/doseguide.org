# MECH Gap Audit — Per-Entry Sourcing

Every mechanism explanation in MECH_GAP_AUDIT.md needs a citable basis.
This document provides per-entry sourcing with confidence levels.

Confidence tiers:
- **DIRECT**: Study examines the specific combination
- **MECHANISTIC**: No study of this exact combo, but mechanism is established
  from individual drug pharmacology in peer-reviewed sources
- **INFERRED**: Mechanism extrapolated from related combinations with shared
  pharmacology (e.g., meth+fentanyl sourced from cocaine+opioid data)

## DANGEROUS — 5 entries

### amphetamines+fentanyl
**Confidence: INFERRED (from stimulant+opioid class data)**
- CDC MMWR 2025: stimulant-involved OD deaths, ~70% co-involving synthetic opioids. Friedman & Shover 2023, Addiction 118(12):2477-2485, DOI: 10.1111/add.16318.
- Temporal unmasking mechanism: Suen et al. 2022, Frontiers in Pharmacology 13:859563, DOI: 10.3389/fphar.2022.859563 — reviews stimulant+opioid neurochemistry and unmasking effect.
- Fentanyl contamination of amphetamine supply: Khatri et al. 2024, Drug Alcohol Depend 254:111020, DOI: 10.1016/j.drugalcdep.2023.111020.

### fentanyl+methamphetamine
**Confidence: DIRECT**
- Glick et al. 2021, Am J Addict 30(3):236-244, DOI: 10.1111/ajad.13128 — "goofball" use doubled 2017-2019 among PWID.
- Ellis et al. 2018, Drug Alcohol Depend 183:279-284 — methamphetamine-opioid co-involvement in overdose deaths.
- CDC MMWR 2025 (74:32): stimulant-involved overdose deaths Jan 2018-Jun 2024.
- Temporal unmasking: Suen et al. 2022 (same as above).

### fentanyl+nitrous
**Confidence: MECHANISTIC**
- N₂O triggers endogenous opioid release: Finck et al. 1995, Anesthesia & Analgesia 80(4):747-752, DOI: 10.1097/00000539-199504000-00017 — N₂O inhalation increased CSF met-enkephalin 28-400x in dogs.
- N₂O analgesia reversed by naloxone: Berkowitz et al. 1976, Science 194(4268):967-968, DOI: 10.1126/science.982058.
- N₂O opioid peptide release in PAG: Emmanouil & Quock 2007, Molecular Neurobiology 25(2):167-189 (review), and Quock et al. 2011, Brain Research 1396:1-7, DOI: 10.1016/j.brainres.2011.04.017.
- N₂O suppresses hypoxic ventilatory drive: Yacoub et al. 1976, Br J Anaesth 48:947-952; Dahan et al. 1996, Anesthesiology 85(1):60-68, DOI: 10.1097/00000542-199607000-00009.
- No direct study of N₂O + fentanyl recreational co-use exists. Mechanism is additive/synergistic opioid pathway convergence.

### ghb+pcp
**Confidence: MECHANISTIC**
- GHB dose-response and respiratory depression: Busardò & Jones 2015, Current Neuropharmacology 13(1):47-70, DOI: 10.2174/1570159X13666141210215423.
- PCP dissociative anesthesia and behavioral effects: Lau et al. 2023, Toxicology Reports 10:249-257 (review).
- No direct study of GHB+PCP combination exists. Mechanism inferred from individual drug pharmacology.

### pcp+tramadol
**Confidence: MECHANISTIC**
- PCP NMDA antagonism potentiating opioid effects: Lodge & Mercier 2015, Drug Discovery Today 20(11):1163-1168 (NMDA-opioid interaction review).
- Tramadol seizure threshold lowering: Rehni et al. 2008, Indian J Pharmacol 40(1):33-36.
- No direct study of PCP+tramadol combination exists.

## UNSAFE — 31 entries

### 2cx+cocaine
**Confidence: MECHANISTIC**
- 2C-B cardiovascular effects and 5-HT2A agonism: Papaseit et al. 2018, Scientific Reports 8:5378, DOI: 10.1038/s41598-018-23609-3 — human pharmacology of 2C-B.
- Cocaine sympathomimetic profile: Schwartz et al. 2010, Cardiology in Review 18(4):177-186.
- Steep 2C-B dose-response: PsychonautWiki/Erowid community consensus; Papaseit confirms clinically.

### 2cx+methamphetamine
**Confidence: MECHANISTIC**
- Same 2C-B pharmacology source as above.
- Meth serotonin/dopamine/NE release: Courtney & Ray 2014, Neuropharmacology 87:171-179, DOI: 10.1016/j.neuropharm.2014.02.016.

### alcohol+amphetamines
**Confidence: DIRECT**
- Mendelson et al. 1995, Clin Pharmacol Ther 57(5):559-568, PMID: 7768079 — human study: meth reduced subjective alcohol effects without reducing BAC, increased HR 24 bpm.
- Peacock et al. 2013, Drug Alcohol Rev 32(6):588-595, DOI: 10.1111/dar.12063 — systematic review of alcohol+amphetamine combination effects.

### alcohol+dxm
**Confidence: MECHANISTIC**

- DXM serotonin reuptake inhibition: Codd et al. 1995, J Pharmacol Exp Ther 274(3):1263-1270, PMID: 7562497.
- Alcohol GABA-A potentiation: Lobo & Harris 2008, Pharmacol Biochem Behav 90(1):90-94, DOI: 10.1016/j.pbb.2008.01.011.

### alcohol+mdma
**Confidence: DIRECT**

### alcohol+nitrous

## WHAT THIS MEANS FOR THE MECH TEXT

The MECH explanations in MECH_GAP_AUDIT.md should NOT themselves contain
inline citations (they're user-facing text that needs to read clearly at 2am).
Instead, the sourcing lives here and in the _src system. For the 3 INFERRED
entries, the text should acknowledge the extrapolation, e.g.:

- amphetamines+fentanyl: says "Stimulant effects mask opioid respiratory depression"
  → sourced to class-level CDC data + Suen 2022 mechanistic review, not
  a study of amphetamine+fentanyl specifically.

For the 26 MECHANISTIC entries, individual drug mechanisms are well-established
even though no one has specifically studied that exact recreational combo.
This is the correct approach for harm reduction — you don't wait for someone
to study "DXM + kratom respiratory depression in humans" before warning people.
You explain the pharmacological basis and cite the individual drug mechanisms.
